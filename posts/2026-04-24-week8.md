## Refining the Audience: Why K-pop?

After establishing the functional requirements in Week 7, I spent this week narrowing down the specific community for my A2 prototype. I have decided to focus on **K-pop fandoms**. 

K-pop communities are unique because they are highly organized and driven by "contribution culture"—fans don't just consume content; they actively participate in "streaming parties," voting, and offline events to support their idols. However, this intensity often leads to toxic "gatekeeping" or the rise of bots that manipulate engagement metrics. By focusing on this niche, my **Fan Qualification System** transforms from a generic scoring tool into a **"Fandom Passport"**—a digital identity that verifies authentic, multi-dimensional support.

## From Requirements to Technical Logic (LO2)

The biggest challenge this week was deciding how to translate my abstract "qualification logic" into a functional web interface using the **Bla+Bla framework**. 

### 1. State Management for Real-time Feedback
To meet the requirement of showing "real-time qualification results," I plan to utilize **React's `useState` hook**. In a K-pop context, when a user clicks a "Simulate Stream" or "Attend Virtual Event" button, the UI needs to update instantly. 
* **Decision:** I chose to use a centralized state to track "Contribution Points."
* **Reasoning:** This allows for a reactive "Progress Bar" that visually fills up as the fan interacts. This immediate feedback loop is crucial for user retention in gamified fandom environments.
