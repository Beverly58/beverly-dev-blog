## Project Direction: Fan Qualification System

In this project, I aim to explore a fan qualification system for online communities, especially within fandom culture.

Many current platforms use visible metrics such as likes, comments, or purchase records to identify “real fans”. However, these metrics are often easy to manipulate and may not reflect genuine engagement.

This creates a situation where fans are judged based on quantity rather than meaningful participation. As a result, some users may feel excluded or pressured to perform certain behaviours.

## Defining the Problem

The key problem is how to define what makes someone a “qualified fan”. This is not only a technical issue, but also a social and design challenge.

For example, should a fan be recognised based on activity, emotional investment, or contribution to the community? Different definitions may lead to very different systems.

## Initial Functional Ideas

At this stage, I identify several possible functions for the system:

- Tracking user behaviour such as posting, commenting, or interacting  
- Assigning a score or level based on these behaviours  
- Providing feedback such as badges or visible status  
- Potentially limiting or unlocking features based on qualification  

These functions suggest that the system is not just a feature, but something that shapes how users interact within the community.

## Considerations

One important consideration is fairness. A simple scoring system may be easy to build but may not accurately represent different types of fans.

Another consideration is user experience. If the system is too complex, users may not understand how their qualification is calculated.

## Next Steps

In the next stage, I will explore different ways to design the qualification system and consider how interaction design can influence user behaviour in a meaningful way.
